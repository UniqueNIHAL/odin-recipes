Brick-Oven Pizza Recipe

Description

This simple brick-oven pizza recipe has been made famous by several well-known, Brooklyn wood-fire pizzerias. Best accompanied by ice-cold, pilsner-style lager beer.

Ingridents

    Dough

  • 1/2 Cup of Water
  • 1 Teaspoon of Yeast
  • 1 Teaspoon Salt
  • 3 Cups bread flour
  • 1 Tablespoon Olive Oil

    • Toppings

  • 6 ounces of mozerella cheese
  • 1 Tea spoon Oregano
  • 3 Basil leaves
  • Veggies of your choice

Steps

  1. Make the dough: Pour warm water into a large bowl, then sprinkle yeast over the top. Let stand for 5 minutes. Stir in cold water and salt. Stir in flour, 1 cup at a time until incorporated.

  2. Make the pizzas: Remove dough from the refrigerator one hour prior to using.

  3. Prepare the first pizza: Lightly dust one dough ball with flour and stretch gradually until about 14 inches in diameter, or about the size of the pizza stone. Place on the floured peel

  4. Lay 1/2 of the mozzarella on the crust, then sprinkle 1/4 teaspoon oregano and 1/8 teaspoon pepper over the top. Randomly arrange 1/4 cup crushed tomatoes over the pizza, leaving some empty areas, then drizzle 1 tablespoon olive oil over top.

  5. Bake in the preheated oven until the crust begins to brown, 4 to 6 minutes.

  6. Slide the pizza onto the peel and remove from the oven; then slide off the peel onto a work surface. Sprinkle 1/2 of the basil leaves randomly over the pizza. Cut into wedges and serve
